How is MITOS affiliated with MIT?

MITOS is a subcommittee of the MIT ACM/IEEE Club. This is the MIT student branch of two international organizations, the Association for Computing Machinery and Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers.

How can I get involved with MITOS as a student?

First, contact us at mitos-exec@mit.edu so we can add you to our mailing list! We’ll notify members via email before our meetings with the agenda for the week. Activities will vary by week, and include panels, open-source project presentations, student speakers, and student work time. Feel free to drop by any week for free food, project inspiration, and student support!

Meeting with guest speakers are recorded and put up on our Youtube channel in case you aren’t able to attend.

Can I present my open source project to MITOS?

Of course! One of our main goals is to connect project owners and contributors with students. Contact us at mitos-exec@mit.edu and we’ll try to get something set up!

If you are remote, please note that at this point, we prefer in-person presentations for active student participation. However, if you let us know when you’ll be in the area, we will work around your schedule.

Does MITOS have a stance on open source vs. free software?

Our primary focus is to help students find projects and provide them support. Beyond that, the club remains neutral on open source vs. free software. Though individual members may have varied opinions on the subject, we are open to all and help anyone who wants to contribute.

However, we do think it’s important to be aware of the social implications of your work. We encourage discussion, so feel free to ask your questions during our meetings!
